---
title: "Viewing & executing SOPs"
description: "Run a procedure step by step and capture evidence that it was followed."
---

Documenting a procedure is only half the story — Process Central lets you **execute** it and record proof that each step was done.

<Frame caption="Viewing and executing an SOP">
  <img src="/images/screenshots/sop-view.png" alt="Executing an SOP step by step" />
</Frame>

## View an SOP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the SOP">
    Select it from the [SOPository](/user-guide/sopository) or the dashboard's recent list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the overview">
    The view shows the purpose, tags, status and the full list of steps with their instructions, screenshots and expected results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Execute an SOP

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start execution">
    From a **published** SOP, begin executing to work through it one step at a time.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Complete each step">
    Follow the instructions, compare against the expected result, and mark the step complete. Where required, capture evidence as you go.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Finish">
    When the final step is complete, the execution is recorded — including who ran it, when, and the outcome.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Every execution contributes to your [Reporting](/user-guide/reporting) and, for administrators, the [Audit & Evidence](/admin/audit-evidence) trail — the proof that your SOPs are actually being used.
</Note>

<Tip>
Executing step by step (rather than reading top to bottom) keeps people on-track for long or safety-critical procedures and produces a complete, defensible record.
</Tip>
